The chiral-induced spin selectivity (CISS) effect is a thought to underlie a broad class of experimental phenomenon involving electrons going through a helical molecule experiencing a spin polarization effect, atypically at room temperature1,2. When electrons are transmitted through a helical molecular junction, the direction of the spin polarization of the outgoing current depends on the chirality of the molecule and the direction of the electron transport, making it possible to generate spin dependent currents. Depending on the handedness, i.e. the chirality, of a molecule, the interaction is more favored or less favored for a given spin polarization
