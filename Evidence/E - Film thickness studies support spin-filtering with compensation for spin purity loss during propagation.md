# E - Film thickness studies support spin-filtering with compensation for spin purity loss during propagation

## Description

Systematic studies varying the thickness of chiral organic films find that spin polarization does not decay simply with distance. The data support a model where spin purity is lost during inter-molecular hopping but is partially compensated by continued spin filtering. This is consistent with the impurity-wavefunction coupling model proposed for biomolecular/semiconductor systems.
