# Q - What role does spin-orbit coupling play in CISS?

## Context

Spin-orbit coupling (SOC) is typically invoked to explain how a chiral electrostatic potential can mix spin states and produce spin-polarized currents. However, in light organic molecules (e.g., DNA, peptides), SOC is expected to be very weak, yet CISS is experimentally robust — creating a long-standing puzzle.
