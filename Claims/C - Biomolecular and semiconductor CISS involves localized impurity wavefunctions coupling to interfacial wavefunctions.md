# C - Biomolecular and semiconductor CISS involves localized impurity wavefunctions coupling to interfacial wavefunctions

## Summary

For CISS observed in biomolecular (e.g., DNA, peptide) and semiconductor systems, the proposed mechanism involves localized impurity or defect states at the molecule-substrate interface whose wavefunctions hybridize with the interfacial electronic states. This hybridization, in the presence of chiral structure, leads to spin-dependent transport.
