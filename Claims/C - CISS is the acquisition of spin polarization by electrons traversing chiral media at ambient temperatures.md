# C - CISS is the acquisition of spin polarization by electrons traversing chiral media at ambient temperatures

## Summary

The CISS (Chirality-Induced Spin Selectivity) effect is defined as the preferential transmission of one spin projection over another when electrons pass through a chiral medium, even at room temperature. This working definition frames the three key components: what counts as "chiral," what "induced" means physically, and what constitutes measurable "spin selectivity."
