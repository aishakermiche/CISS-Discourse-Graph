# P - Foo et al. 2025 - Mind the Gap

**Full Title:** Mind the Gap: From Resolving Theoretical Foundations of Chiral(ity)-Induced Spin Selectivity to Pioneering Implementations in Quantum Sensing

**Authors:** Yan Xi Foo, Aisha Kermiche, Farhan T. Chowdhury, Clarice D. Aiello, Luke D. Smith

**Journal:** Chemical Physics Reviews (AIP Publishing), Vol. 6, No. 3, Article 031306 (2025)

**arXiv:** 2508.05611

## Summary

A comprehensive review of the CISS (Chirality-Induced Spin Selectivity) effect that:
1. Clarifies what constitutes the "chiral," the "induced," and the "spin-selectivity" in CISS
2. Surveys key theoretical model realizations
3. Examines emerging quantum sensing applications with emphasis on spin-correlated radical pairs
4. Argues that different mechanisms operate in different material systems (biomolecular, solid-state, chiral metals)

## Claims Made

- [[C - CISS is the acquisition of spin polarization by electrons traversing chiral media at ambient temperatures]]
- [[C - Helical geometry is the primary geometric source of CISS rather than general chirality]]
- [[C - A single unified mechanism cannot account for CISS across all experimental systems]]
- [[C - Biomolecular and semiconductor CISS involves localized impurity wavefunctions coupling to interfacial wavefunctions]]
- [[C - This coupling mechanism breaks Onsager-Casimir reciprocity via magnetization-dependent barrier-potential adjustments]]
- [[C - CISS in chiral metals respects Onsager-Casimir relations unlike biomolecular and semiconductor systems]]
- [[C - Molecular knots exhibit up to 90% spin polarization]]
- [[C - Spin-filtered electron currents propagate over hundreds of nanometers in mesoscopic films]]
- [[C - Spin-correlated radical pairs offer a tunable biomimetic platform for molecular quantum technologies]]
