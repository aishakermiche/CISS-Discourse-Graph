abstract: "Originating at the intersection of physics and biosensing, quantum biosensors ({QB}) are transforming medical diagnostics and personalized medicine by exploiting quantum phenomena to amplify sensitivity, specificity, and detection speed compared to traditional biosensors. Their foundation lies in the fusion of biological entities like {DNA}, proteins, or enzymes with quantum sensors, which elicits discernible alterations in light emissions when interacting with sample molecules. Their prowess in identifying disease-linked biomarkers presents an avenue for early diagnoses of conditions like Alzheimer’s and cancer. Beyond this, they enable real-time monitoring of treatment responses by capturing the dynamism of biomarkers, but {QB} still faces challenges, such as issues of stability, reproducibility, and intricate quantum interactions. Moreover, seamless integration into prevailing diagnostic frameworks necessitates careful consideration. Looking ahead, the evolution of {QB} navigates uncharted territories. Innovations in fabrication techniques, interdisciplinary collaborations, and standardization protocols emerge as pivotal areas of exploration. This comprehensive discourse encapsulates {QB}’s principles, diverse iterations, and burgeoning medical utilities. It delves into inherent challenges and limitations, shedding light on the potential trajectories of future research. As {QB} continues to evolve, its potential to redefine medical diagnostics becomes increasingly tangible. The saga of {QB} resonates with possibilities, poised to reshape the diagnostic landscape profoundly. , Highlights Highlighting various mechanisms and strategies to develop quantum biosensors, harmoniously united with biomolecules, triggers profound changes in light emissions, thereby fostering a new realm of diagnostic insights. Expressing the myriad applications of quantum biosensors that we discuss encompass the early detection of disease-linked biomarkers and real-time monitoring of treatment responses. Addressing the challenges intrinsic to the odyssey of quantum biosensors, encompassing stability, reproducibility, and intricate quantum interactions. Summarizing our manuscript forges ahead to map potential future trajectories of Quantum biosensors, revolutionize medical diagnostics with unmatched sensitivity, real-time monitoring, and potential for personalized treatment, promising improved patient outcomes worldwide."
